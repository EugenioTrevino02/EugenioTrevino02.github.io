Cinema Through Time: How Seasons and History Shape the Movies We Watch

Movies Through the Lens of History and Seasons

Cinema has always reflected societal moods and historical moments, offering a window into our past and a glimpse of imagined futures. Our project analyzes how historical events and seasons influence the themes and genres of films.

Introduction

Throughout history, cinema has served as both a mirror and a window reflecting societal moods while offering glimpses into imagined futures. From timeless classics like “A Bridge Too Far” that show us the atrocities of war, to feel-good rom-coms like “How to lose a guy in 10 days” tied to valentines, movies have always been influenced by the times and seasons in which they were created or consumed. This intersection between cultural phenomena and cinematic storytelling is what makes films not only a form of art but also a rich source of historical and social insight.

From the trenches of world wars to the joy of Christmas mornings, historical events and seasonal moments shape the stories filmmakers tell and the ones audiences want to hear. But how significant is this influence? Does the timing of a movie's release—whether during summer blockbusters, Halloween scares, or Valentine's romance—affect its themes and popularity? And do historical events such as wars leave indelible marks on the genres of films being produced?

Movies Through the Lens of History and Seasons

Our project aims to uncover these patterns by analyzing the relationship between key historical events and movie trends, as well as the seasonal influence on cinema. Do war films surge during times of conflict? Is there a noticeable uptick in family or heartwarming movies during the holidays? By digging into these questions, we hope to paint a vivid picture of how cinema has evolved in response to the world around it.

Research Questions

  • How do major historical events shape the genres and themes of movies?
  • What seasonal trends exist in the popularity of specific movie genres?
  • How does the sentiment in plot summaries reflect major historical events?
  • How do historical periods and seasonal timing influence movie budgets?
  • What impact do release season and historical context have on a movie's box office revenue?

About Our Data

Our analysis was made using two primary datasets: the CMU Dataset and the TMDB Movies Dataset 2024 from Kaggle. Each dataset offers unique strengths that, when combined, provide a rich foundation for exploring the connections between cinema, history, and seasonal trends.

The CMU Dataset serves as our foundational source, offering detailed information on genres, release dates, and thematic elements. To complement this dataset, we incorporated the TMDB Movies Dataset, a comprehensive database of 1 million movies enriched with metadata such as ratings, revenue, and popularity scores; this dataset is particularly valuable for filling in missing values in revenue and popularity fields within the CMU Dataset. By integrating TMDB data, we were able to standardize genres and release dates across datasets, ensuring consistency and enhancing overall data quality as well as diving deeper into the financial and audience reception aspects of cinema. By combining the strengths of both datasets, we aim to provide a robust and accurate exploration of cinema’s evolution over time.

The first step in our data cleaning process is to identify any missing values and decide on the appropriate strategy for handling them. Missing values can affect our analysis and models, so we either fill them in with suitable values or remove the affected rows if necessary.

Features of our cleaned dataset in numbers:

  • wikipedia_id - A unique identifier for the movie's Wikipedia page
  • freebase_id - A unique identifier used by Freebase
  • name - The title of the movie
  • languages - A list of languages spoken in the movie
  • countries - The list of countries where the movie was produced or released
  • budget - The total production cost of the movie
  • popularity - A numeric value indicating the movie's popularity from TMDB website
  • revenue - The total amount of money the movie earned from all sources
  • runtime - The total length of the movie in minutes
  • genre - The categories or types of the movie
  • release_date - The official date when the movie was released
